The AI-Generated Site

You used ChatGPT, Bolt, or v0 to build your site. And honestly, it came out pretty good — at first. But now you need to change something, and every time you ask the AI to fix one thing, it breaks two others. The code is a mystery. The layout shifts every time you touch it. You just want someone who can look at what's there, understand it, and make it right.

This is not your fault. AI tools can generate code fast, but they don't understand your site as a whole. They're pattern-matching, not problem-solving. You need a developer who can read what the AI wrote and fix it with intention.

{{- /* Scenario 2: Page Builder Wall */}}

02

The Page Builder Wall

You built your site on Squarespace, Wix, or WordPress with a visual editor. It worked fine for a while. But now you need something the builder won't let you do — a specific layout, a booking widget that actually works, a form that sends to the right place — and you've hit a wall. You've watched the YouTube tutorials. You've tried the workarounds. You're done.

This is genuinely hard. Page builders are designed for the 80% case. The moment you need something outside that box, you're fighting the tool instead of building your business. We can step in, do the one thing you need, and get out of your way.

{{- /* Scenario 3: Inherited Site */}}

03

The Inherited Site

Someone built you a WordPress site a few years ago — a nephew, a friend's kid, a freelancer who's since disappeared. It worked fine until it didn't. Now there's a security warning, the contact form stopped sending emails six months ago, or the whole thing loads so slowly that customers give up before the page appears. You don't know who to call because the person who built it is gone.

You're not stuck. We work on sites we didn't build all the time. We'll look at what's there, tell you honestly what it needs, and give you a clear price to fix it.
